RECOMMENDED ACTIONS
-------------------
Actions to protect human life and property
different in tsunami warning areas.

If you are in a tsunami advisory area;

* Move out of the water, off the beach, and away from the beach
   harbors, marinas, breakwaters, bays and entrances.

* Be vigilant and follow directions from your local
   emergency officials because they may have more details or
   information specific to your location.

* If you feel a strong earthquake or the ground rolls for a long time
   take immediate protective actions such as moving into land
   and/or uphill, preferably on foot.

* Boat operators,
     * When time and conditions allow moving your boat out
       sea to a depth of at least 180 feet.

* If staying at sea, avoid entering shallow waters, harbors,
       marinas, bays and entrances to avoid floating and
       submerged debris and strong currents.

* Do not go ashore to observe the tsunami.

* Do not return to shore until local emergency officials
   indicates it is safe to do so.

AFFECTS
-------
The impact will vary in different locations in the advisory
Area.

If you are in a tsunami advisory area;

* A tsunami with strong waves and currents can occur.

* Waves and currents may drown or injure people
   in the country.

* Flows at beaches and in harbors, marinas,
   bays and entrances can be especially dangerous.

* Some impacts may continue for hours to days later
   the arrival of the first wave.

* The first wave may not be the largest so later waves may
   larger.

* Each wave can last from 5 to 45 minutes when an invasive wave
   and retreat.

* Coasts facing all directions are threatened because
   Waves can surround islands, headlands and into bays.

* Strong shaking or rolling of the ground indicates
   An earthquake has already occurred and a tsunami may be imminent.

* Coastline receding rapidly or retreating, abnormal waves and
   Sound and strong currents are signs of a tsunami.

* Tsunamis can appear when water moves quickly into the sea,
   a rising tide is gentle like a flood without breaking waves,
   like a series of broken waves, or a wall of bubbling water.

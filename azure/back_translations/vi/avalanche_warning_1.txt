
NEWSLETTER - REQUEST BROADCAST IMMEDIATELY
Warning of avalanches in remote areas
Utah Avalanche Center Forestry Department Salt Lake City UT
Forwarded by Salt Lake City UT National Weather Service
641 AM MST Sunday, February 16, 2025

... THE AVALANCHE CENTER OF THE UTAH DEPARTMENT OF FORESTRY HAS CONTINUED A 
REMOTE AVALANCHE WARNING IN EFFECT FROM 6 A.M. MST 
SUNDAY TO MONDAY 6 A.M. MST...

*SOMETHING.. The avalanche risk for the warning area is HIGH. 

*WHERE... For most of Utah's mountains, including 
  Wasatch Mountains, Uinta Mountains, Wasatch Plateau, Manti Skyline, 
  Abajos, and the Tushar Mountains. 

*WHEN... Effective from 6 a.m. MST this morning to 6 a.m. MST Monday. 

*IMPACT ON... Heavy snowfall and drifting due to strong winds created
  areas of unstable snow and avalanches are dangerous
  conditions at all altitudes. Natural and man-made
  Avalanches are certain. People should avoid traveling
  avalanche terrain and avoid avalanches. Avoided
  on or under terrain steep more than 30 degrees. 

PREVENTIVE/PREPARATORY ACTIONS...

Stay away from and out under slopes more than 30 degrees.

Visitors to remote areas should consult www.utahavalanchecenter.org
or call 1-888-999-4019 for more information. 

This warning does not apply to ski areas at risk of avalanches
mitigation measures are taken.

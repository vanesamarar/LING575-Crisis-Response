Backcountry Avalanche Warning
Forest Service Utah Avalanche City Salt Lake UT Center
Forwarded by Salt Lake City National Weather Service, UT
641 am MST Sun Feb 16, 2025

... UTAH AVALANCHE FOREST SERVICE CENTER CONTINUED 
AVALANCHE BACKCOUNTRY WARNING COMING INTO FORCE FROM 6 A.M. MST 
Sunday to 6 a.m. MST, Monday...

* What. The danger of rainfall for the warning area is high. 

* Where... For most of Utah's mountains, including 
  Wasatch Range, Uinta Mountain, Wasatch Plateau, Manti Skyline, 
  Abajos, and Tushar Ranges. 

* When... Effective from 6 a.m. MST this morning to 6 a.m. MST Monday. 

* Impact... Heavy snow falling and drifting by strong winds created
  Widespread areas of unstable snow and dangerous slips
  Conditions at all heights. Nature and man-made causes
  People should avoid traveling altogether
  Avalanche Terrain and keep out of avalanche runs. Avoid
  Above or below steep slopes of more than 30 degrees. 

Action Caution/Readiness...

Stay off and out under a slope of over 30 degrees.

Backcountry travelers should consult www.utahavalanchecenter.org
or call 1-888-999-4019 for more details. 

This warning does not apply to ski areas where there is a risk of rainfall
Mitigation measures have been implemented.

Recommended actions
-------------------
Actions to protect human life and property
Varies in the tsunami advisory area.

If you are in a tsunami advisory area;

* Move away from the water, off the beach and away from
   The port, marina, water split, coast and entrance.

* Be cautious and follow the instructions from your base
   Emergency officials as they can be more detailed, or
   Specific information for your location

* If you feel a strong earthquake or additional rolling
   Take immediate preventive measures such as changes in the territory
   and/or hiking on foot.

* Boat operators,
     * Where time and conditions allow moving your boat out
       The sea reaches a depth of at least 180 feet.

* If at sea avoid going into shallow water, harbor,
       marinas, bays, and inlets to avoid floating and
       Submerged debris and strong currents.

* Don't go to the coast to observe the tsunami.

* Do not return to the coast until local emergency officials
   Indicates that it is safe to do so.

Impact
-------
Ukuhlaziywa kwezinhlobo ezingaphezulu
Area.

If you are in a tsunami advisory area;

* Tsunami waves with strong waves and currents are possible.

* Waves and currents may sink or injure people who are
   in water.

* Navigation at Marina Beach and Harbor
   The beaches and entrances can be especially dangerous.

* Some side effects may last for several hours to days after
   The arrival of the first wave.

* The first wave may not be the biggest, so the later wave may
   Larger.

* Each wave can last from 5 to 45 minutes when the wave violates
   and decrease.

* Coastlines facing all directions are threatened due to
   Waves can wrap around islands and parcels and into the coast.

* Strong tremors or landslides indicate that:
   An earthquake has occurred and a tsunami may be imminent.

* Rapidly shrinking coastlines, unusual waves, and
   Strong sounds and currents are signs of a tsunami.

* Tsunami waves can appear as rapidly moving water into the sea,
   The tide is gentle like a flood without breaking waves,
   A series of breaking waves, or frothy water walls.
